The Surface of Mars

STARS

The first problems started when the red planet’s molten core solidified, putting an end to plate tectonics as well as the magnetic field that protected the planet. The magnetic field is important because it diverts a large amount of the Sun’s ionizing radiation, protecting the delicate balance of the planet below. Without this field, these charged particles collide with the atmosphere and react with it, slowly stripping it away over the course of billions of years in a way comparable to sandpaper. Depending on how much water was in the atmosphere at this point, Mars could have lost between 10 and 70% of its water this way.
